Tech Industry Issues, Where it Fails to Innovate

Sep 18th, 2014

In the last days as part of my participation in DevBootcamp I conducted some research, and considered as a technology lover what are the biggest issues that affect this marvelous industry.
One main aspect that has been discussed in depth and continues to be considered is the diversity issue.

It was just last July that Twitter released their first "diversity report" and there have been several articles pointing this report mainly addressing the issues of race and gender inequality.
The numbers speak for themselves: 90% of global employees in Technology are men, with only 10% women. As for ethnicity 58% are white and 34% are Asian.
The other part of the numbers that I find interesting is that Tech as an industry does not seem to be a the best example of diversity.

In taking time considering how we got here, it has become clear that this is simply the result of the education that we have been subjected to as human culture.
At a most fundamental level it's a matter of how the majority of the people still identifies themselves and others with outdated ideas of gender, roles, expectations, just because we were born in a specific location, with a specific ethnicity and gender.

So what can we do really?
It just happens that this and so many other issues that we experience as the people of the world are very important to me. I decided long time ago that what is most important is to live a life in complete openness, gratitude, and kindness without limiting myself in this for any reason not for any idea or concept! I made this my lifestyle and this is what I aspire to be in every interaction I have with all beings.

I am supported and inspired to live a life that is of benefit to all by Balanced View, and I feel blessed to share such an enriching existence with countless friends all over the world
